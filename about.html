History of Zongzi

Let’ learn more about history of Zongzi.

The Story Behind Zongzi

Qu Yuan was a loyal poet and minister in ancient China. When he saw his country fall into danger, he was very sad and jumped into the river on the 5th day of the 5th lunar month. To honor him, people threw rice dumplings (zongzi) into the river to feed the fish and paddled dragon boats to search for his body. These acts became the Dragon Boat Festival traditions.

When do we eat Zongzi?

Fifth Day of the Fifth Lunar Month.

Symbolic Meanings

Zongzi represents remembrance, protection, and patriotism. Making and eating it brings families together and keeps cultural traditions alive.

Fun Fact about Dragon Boat Festival

(1) Dragon Boat Racing

After Qu Yuan threw himself into the Miluo River, the local residents, in an effort to recover his body, built boats to search for him in the river. This eventually evolved into the dragon boat races we have today.

(2) Eating Zongzi

After Qu Yuan's death, local residents threw rice wrapped in bamboo leaves into the river to prevent the fish and shrimp from consuming his body. This practice has evolved into the tradition of eating zongzi during the Dragon Boat Festival

(3) Egg Balancing

On the day of the Dragon Boat Festival, the Yang energy is strongest. If you can balance an egg at noon, it is said to symbolize good luck and fortune for the coming year.

(4) Hanging Mugwort and Calamus

In the past, people would hang mugwort and calamus at their doorways to ward off poison and evil spirits. It is believed that these plants are sacred and have protective powers.

(5) Fragrant Sachets

Fragrant sachets were once considered symbols of good fortune, and wearing one was believed to protect the wearer from harmful or evil influences.

(6) Drinking Realgar Wine

Realgar wine is believed to have the ability to ward off epidemics. It contains rich minerals, and when mixed with rice wine, it is seen as a medicinal remedy. Although children should not drink alcohol, drawing the character "王" (king) on their foreheads with realgar wine is said to have protective powers against evil.

(7) Collecting Noon Water

"Noon water" refers to well water or spring water collected at noon during the Dragon Boat Festival, as this time of year has the strongest Yang energy. It is believed to have powerful protective, purifying, and fortune-enhancing qualities, and is also used to cool down during the summer.
