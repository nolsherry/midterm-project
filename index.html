What is Zongzi?

Zongzi is a traditional Chinese rice dumpling made of glutinous rice filled with savory or sweet ingredients, wrapped in bamboo leaves, and steamed or boiled. It is especially eaten during the Dragon Boat Festival, which falls on the 5th day of the 5th month in the lunar calendar. Zongzi varies by region—northern versions tend to be more savory and feature stir-fried rice, while southern ones are softer and boiled with raw rice. Common fillings include pork belly, mushrooms, salted egg yolks, and chestnuts. Zongzi is more than food—it’s a cultural symbol of remembrance, family, and tradition.

Types of Zongzi

Northern-style Zongzi

Zongzi made with stir-fried glutinous rice, resulting in separate grains with a rich flavor.

Southern-style Zongzi

Zongzi made with uncooked glutinous rice, wrapped and then cooked, resulting in a sticky, mellow texture.

Hakka-style Zongzi

Zongzi often filled with preserved vegetables, salted radish, or other savory ingredients.

Sweet-style Zongzi

Zongzi with no filling, usually served with sugar, honey, or other sweet toppings, with a chewy texture.

Step 3: Stir-fry the Glutinous Rice

  • Add oil to a wok.
  • Stir in glutinous rice until fragrant.
  • Season with soy sauce, five-spice powder, and rice wine.
  • Cook until rice is evenly colored. Set aside.

Step 4: Prepare the Fillings

  • Pork Belly: Pan-fry until aromatic, then simmer with soy sauce, sugar, and rice wine until fully seasoned.
  • Shiitake Mushrooms & Dried Shrimp: Stir-fry separately until fragrant.
  • Chestnuts: Blanch briefly to remove bitterness (or pre-cook them if desired).

Step 5: Prepare the Bamboo Leaves

  • Rinse bamboo leaves thoroughly.
  • Blanch in hot water for 5 minutes.
  • Wipe dry and set aside.

Step 6: Wrap the Zongzi

  • Fold two leaves into a cone.
  • Fill with rice, then add pork, mushroom, shrimp, and chestnut.
  • Cover with more rice, press firmly, and tie with string.

Step 7: Steam the Zongzi

  • Place wrapped zongzi in a steamer.
  • Steam for 1.5–2 hours over medium-high heat until fully cooked.

Step 8: Enjoy!

  • Best eaten hot.
  • Optional sauces: sweet chili or garlic soy sauce.
